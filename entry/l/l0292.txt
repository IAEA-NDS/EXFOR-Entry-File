           identify the reactions of interest in the data.        L0292001   40 
           Protons and alpha particles with sufficient energy     L0292001   41 
           from the reaction traveled through the front dE        L0292001   42 
           detector and stopped in the E detector, depositing     L0292001   43 
           some energy in each, such that standard energy loss    L0292001   44 
           techniques could be used for particle identification.  L0292001   45 
           For lower-energy charged particles that stopped in     L0292001   46 
           the dE detector, a separate spectrum was produced      L0292001   47 
           using the E detector as an active veto. In this        L0292001   48 
           technique, the known reaction kinematics were used to  L0292001   49 
           identify the region of interest for each reaction      L0292001   50 
           ejectile. For each incident gamma beam energy, the     L0292001   51 
           total number of proton or alpha events for each of     L0292001   52 
           these techniques was summed to determine raw yields.   L0292001   53 
           Converting these yields to cross sections was          L0292001   54 
           accomplished using the measured Zn target thickness,   L0292001   55 
           detection efficiency, and gamma beam intensity as      L0292001   56 
           determined from the downstream fission chamber.        L0292001   57
